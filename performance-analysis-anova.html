Performance Analysis by ANOVA

Tue 26 June 2018

Question¶

Is there reason to believe that panel manufacturer X's quality is changing year-to-year?

Method¶

First of all, the calculation of Performance Ratio(PR) helps eliminate seasonality within data. Therefore comparing means of 3 vintage groups will find if there is change in manufacturer X's quality. We can conclude that the quality changes year-to-year if the means are significantly different. Otherwise, the quality stays stable.

One-way ANOVA(analysis of variance) is an appropriate way to compare more than 2 populations. It compares the variation between groups to the variation within groups. We can conclude that the observed difference of means is not dut to randomness but the real effect of groups if the virance between groups is significantly bigger than the variance within groups.

ANOVA has the following hypotheses:

H0: $\mu_A=\mu_B=\mu_c$

H1: two or more means are different from the others

We will get a p-value from the procedure and reject the null hypothese if the p-value is valid.

If our ANOVA test shows that the means are not all equal we can further implement Tukey HSD test to find which means are exactly different.

Result & Conclusion¶

After the ANOVA test, the p-value is nearly 1 which indicates we cannot reject the null hypotheses. To conclude, panel manufacturer X's quality stays steady year-to-year.

k = len(data['vintage'].unique())

Calculate Sum of Squares Total¶

This is the total variability in the data with degree of freedom N-1

$SS_{total} = \sum x^2 - N\bar{x}^2$

In [6]:
SSt = data['PR'].var()
df_t = N - 1

Calculate Sum of Squares Between and Mean Squre Between¶

Sum of Squares Between is the variability due to interaction between the vintages with degree of freedom k-1. Sometimes known as the Sum of Squares of the Model

$SS_{between}=\sum n_j\bar{x}_j^2-N\bar{x}^2$

where $\bar{x}_j$ is the mean of each vintage and $n_j$ is the sample size of each vintage.

$MS_{between} = \frac{SS_{between}}{df_B}$

In [7]:
SSb = sum(data.groupby('vintage').count()['PR'] * data.groupby('vintage').mean()['PR']**2) - N * data['PR'].mean()**2
df_b = k - 1
MSb = SSb / df_b

Calculate Sum of Squares Within and Mean Square Within¶

The variability in the data due to differences within system with degree of freedom N-k.

$SS_{within} = SS_{total} - SS_{between}$

$MS_{within} = \frac{SS_{within}}{df_W}$

In [8]:
SSw = SSt - SSb
df_w = N - k
MSw = SSw / df_w
